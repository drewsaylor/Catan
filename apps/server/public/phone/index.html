    Rule Book

    Overview

    • Be the first player to reach the Victory Point (VP) target to win.
    • Earn VP by building settlements (1 VP), cities (2 VP), holding Longest Road (2 VP), Largest Army (2 VP), and VP development cards.

    Setup

    • Each player places 2 settlements and 2 roads during setup.
    • Round 1: Players place in turn order. Round 2: Reverse order.
    • Settlements cannot be placed adjacent to another settlement.
    • Roads must connect to your settlement.
    • After Round 2, you receive starting resources from your second settlement's adjacent hexes.

    Turn Structure

    • Roll Dice: Roll two dice. All players with settlements/cities on hexes matching the number receive resources.
    • Trade: Propose trades with other players or trade with the bank.
    • Build: Spend resources to build roads, settlements, cities, or buy development cards.
    • End Turn: Pass play to the next player.

    Resources

    • Wood: From forest hexes. Used for roads and settlements.
    • Brick: From hill hexes. Used for roads and settlements.
    • Sheep: From pasture hexes. Used for settlements and dev cards.
    • Wheat: From field hexes. Used for settlements, cities, and dev cards.
    • Ore: From mountain hexes. Used for cities and dev cards.
    • Desert hexes produce nothing.

    Building

    • Road (1 Wood + 1 Brick): Place on an edge connected to your network. Needed for Longest Road.
    • Settlement (1 Wood + 1 Brick + 1 Sheep + 1 Wheat): Place on a vertex connected to your road, not adjacent to another settlement. Worth 1 VP. Produces 1 resource per adjacent hex.
    • City (2 Wheat + 3 Ore): Upgrade an existing settlement. Worth 2 VP. Produces 2 resources per adjacent hex.
    • Development Card (1 Sheep + 1 Wheat + 1 Ore): Draw from the deck. Cannot be played the same turn purchased.

    Trading

    • Player Trade: On your turn, propose trades with any combination of resources. Other players can accept or decline.
    • Bank Trade (4:1): Trade 4 of any one resource for 1 of any other.
    • 3:1 Port: If you have a settlement/city on a 3:1 port, trade any 3 of one resource for 1 of any other.
    • 2:1 Port: Trade 2 of the specific resource shown for 1 of any other.

    Development Cards

    • Knight: Move the robber and steal from an adjacent player. Counts toward Largest Army.
    • Road Building: Place 2 roads for free.
    • Year of Plenty: Take any 2 resources from the bank.
    • Monopoly: Name a resource. All players must give you all cards of that type.
    • Victory Point: Worth 1 VP. Kept hidden until you win.
    • You may only play 1 development card per turn (except VP cards).

    The Robber

    • Rolling 7: No resources are produced. Instead, the robber activates.
    • Discard Rule: Any player with 8 or more resource cards must discard half (rounded down).
    • Move Robber: The current player moves the robber to any hex (not where it was).
    • Steal: Steal 1 random card from a player with a settlement/city on that hex.
    • While the robber is on a hex, it does not produce resources.

    Special Bonuses

    • Longest Road (2 VP): Awarded to the first player with 5+ continuous road segments. If another player builds a longer road, they take it.
    • Largest Army (2 VP): Awarded to the first player who plays 3+ Knight cards. If another player plays more knights, they take it.

    Winning

    • The first player to reach or exceed the VP target on their turn wins.
    • Hidden VP cards are revealed when claiming victory.
    • The game ends immediately when a winner is declared.
